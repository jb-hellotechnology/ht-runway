The challenge

An old site that visitors couldn't book from.

Harbourside had a website built years ago that looked dated on a phone — exactly where most of their summer visitors were searching. There was no way to reserve a table online, so every booking meant a phone call during the lunchtime rush, and plenty were missed.

The owner wanted something that looked as good as the food, was easy to keep up to date, and quietly took bookings while the team got on with running the café.

The approach

A fresh, mobile-first site with bookings built in.

I designed a warm, appetising site around their photography, with the menu and an online booking form front and centre. Everything was built mobile-first, so it loads fast and looks great on the phones people actually use down at the harbour.

I set up the local SEO so they show up when visitors search “café in Whitby”, and showed the team how to swap the menu and photos themselves in a couple of minutes.
